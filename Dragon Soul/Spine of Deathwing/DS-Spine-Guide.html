Overview

Spine of Deathwing is the seventh boss of Dragon Soul. The raid fights on Deathwing's back along three plates (sections). Each plate has Corruptions (tendon armor) that must be destroyed so the tendon is exposed; then the raid kills the tendon. Blood adds spawn and must be fused into Amalgamations; when an Amalgamation is killed in the right spot, Deathwing rolls, and players must control the roll (grip the spine or move to the correct side) so no one falls off. After three tendons are destroyed (one per plate), the encounter is complete. Roll control is the key to survival.

⚠
Roll control [N/H]
When Deathwing rolls, players can fall off his back and die. The roll is triggered by killing an Amalgamation in the correct position. The raid must grip the spine (use the grip ability / stand in the safe zone) or move to the side that stays up during the roll. If too many players fall off, the encounter fails. One person (or the whole raid) must trigger the roll at the right time and everyone must be in position to survive it.

Phases & Mechanics (per plate)

Corruptions & tendon [N/H]

  • Each plate has Corruptions (fiery tentacles) that cover the tendon. Kill three Corruptions and stand in the pits (the fiery spots left where they died) so the tendon is exposed and you can DPS it. Leave the fourth Corruption alone (or as your strategy requires).
  • Once three are down and the raid is in the pits, the Burning Tendon is exposed â€” burn it in the short window. Balance the plate so Deathwing doesn't roll from uneven weight; stacking in the pits helps control roll.

Blood adds & Amalgamation [N/H]

  • Blood adds (e.g. Burning Blood) spawn and can fuse into an Amalgamation. Tanks position so blood pools and the Amalgamation forms where you want it for the roll.
  • When the Amalgamation dies it uses Nuclear Blast â€” move away to avoid heavy damage. Kill it in the correct location so that when Deathwing rolls, the raid is on the "up" side and can grip the spine.
  • Weight balance matters: too much weight on one side can cause an unintended roll. Stack in the pits when burning the tendon to control balance.
⚠
Roll: Before killing the Amalgamation, the entire raid must be in position to grip the spine (use the grip ability or stand in the safe zone). Call "grip" or "roll" so everyone is ready. After the roll, Deathwing settles and you can continue to the next step (tendon burn or move to next plate).

Roll control [N/H]

  • When Deathwing rolls, one side of his back goes down and the other goes up. Players on the wrong side fall off and die.
  • Players must use the grip ability (given at the start of the encounter) to hold onto the spine, or move to the side that will be "up" during the roll. The roll direction can be predictable based on where the Amalgamation was killed.
  • Coordinate: one caller announces "roll â€” grip now" so everyone uses grip or moves. Never kill the Amalgamation until the raid is ready.
